Academic Writing

Philosopher with digital media focus

Over the course of my academic career, my writing has increasingly gravitated toward the digital. I engage with topics such as dating apps, online information ecosystems, and digital communities and subcultures, drawing on theoretical frameworks from thinkers including Jean Baudrillard, Susan Sontag, Marshall McLuhan, Simone de Beauvoir, Hegel, Marx, Sartre and the like. While this has occasionally involved admittedly generous interpretations of essay prompts, I am grateful to have built an archive of work that reflects my sustained and evolving philosophical orientation toward digital life.

My paper Body Projects: The Politics of Gym Culture through a Beauvoirian Lens was published in the Fall 2024 edition of the McGill Journal of Human Behaviour. The paper examines the proliferation of contemporary “gym culture”, much of it mediated online, and mobilizes Simone de Beauvoir’s figure of the narcissist to theorize the affective and political dynamics underlying this phenomenon. I am currently in the editing stages of publishing other papers.
