Montreal AI Ethics Institute

UX Designer

I joined the Montreal AI Ethics Institute without a formal title, motivated by a desire to contribute to an organization dedicated to making AI ethics accessible to the public. While I initially positioned myself as a general support resource, my role quickly crystallized into that of a UX designer. MAIEI already produced rigorous, thoughtful content, but in an increasingly crowded digital landscape, I recognized that how information is presented is just as important as the information itself. This was a gap I was well positioned to help address.

In addition to redesigning and restructuring key areas of the website, I developed a taxonomy of article topics and used large language models and Hugging Face tools to categorize MAIEI’s archive of 900+ articles, validating classifications using confidence scores. These changes enabled a significantly improved search experience, including topic-based browsing. Planned expansions include a chatbot-driven search interface, audience-specific content pathways (e.g., “for educators” or “for policymakers”), and new community features such as a job board and discussion forums.

My most significant contribution was the redesign of MAIEI’s flagship State of AI Ethics Report. Previous editions were distributed primarily as standalone PDFs; for the seventh edition, I designed and implemented an interactive, fully navigable web experience with an emphasis on accessibility and usability. The new format includes structured navigation and a dedicated contributors page to highlight the breadth of voices behind the report. The response from readers and collaborators has been overwhelmingly positive, and I am proud to have helped make critical work in AI ethics more accessible to a wider audience.
